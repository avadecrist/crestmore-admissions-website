*(Phone picked up.)*

*(Beat.)*

**Shaira:** Hello?

*(Beat.)*

**Shaira:** Hello?

*(Click. The line goes dead.)*

---

## Scene 1 – Crestmore Admission Office, Morning

*(Typical office sounds. Footsteps approach.)*

**Sam:** What are you doing here so early?

**Lucy (whispering):** Meeting with Sara.

**Sam (whispering):** No way, same.

**Lucy:** Do you think it's because we were on the phone buying concert tickets off that random guy last week?

**Sam:** I didn't even think of that. I thought she heard me tell you about my freaky date from last week...

**Lucy:** Well, shit.

*(Door opens. Sara enters.)*

**Sara:** Lucy, let’s have a little chat. Shall we?

**Lucy:** Uh, yeah. Sure.

*(Footsteps. Clock ticking.)*

---

## Scene 2 – Later

*(Door opens. Footsteps as Lucy returns.)*

**Sara:** I’ll be back for you in a second, Sam.

**Sam:** Sounds good!

*(Door shuts.)*

**Sam (eager):** What did she say?!

**Lucy:** She knows we’re slacking off and that you answer all the phones and I do all the emails and interviews.

**Sam:** Great.

**Lucy:** So she’s separating us today.

**Sam:** No!

**Lucy:** Yes. You’re on emails and interviews and I’m… on phones.

**Sam:** Oh god, are you ready for that?

**Lucy:** I don’t think I can do it.

**Sam:** You got this. Stay calm. Use the cheat sheet.

*(Phone rings.)*

**Lucy:** Oh, shit!

*(Door opens.)*

**Sara:** Come on in, Sam.

**Sam (to Lucy):** You got this!

*(Footsteps. Door shuts.)*

*(Phone rings again. Lucy answers.)*

---

## Scene 3 – The Call

**Lucy (hesitant):** Crestmore University Office of Admission, this is Lucy speaking. How can I help you?

**Ken:** Hello, ma’am. I was just calling with a few quick questions. Am I calling the right place?

**Lucy:** Yeah, uh, totally! I’m assuming you have questions about a prospective student?

**Ken:** That’s absolutely right. My son just got his acceptance from Crestmore. We are thrilled.

**Lucy:** Oh, well congratulations — to your son! You called the right place. I’d be happy to help.

**Ken:** Fantastic. I just had some questions about Crestmore’s student living situation.

**Lucy:** Sure! Crestmore requires first-year students to live in dorms. All freshmen are split across two residence halls—

*(Papers rustle.)*

**Ken:** Aren’t there three residence halls? Smith, Wilson, and Miller?

**Lucy (nervous):** Ah — right. Three dorms.

*(More papers rustling.)*

**Ken:** And who exactly will be my son’s roommate?

**Lucy:** Great question! Roommates are automatically assigned and your son will find out about a month before school starts.

**Ken:** Well how can I get to know a little more about this person?

**Lucy:** I’m not sure what you’re asking.

**Ken:** Can’t you provide more information? I thought I called the right place.

**Lucy:** He’ll receive an email with his roommate’s name and number so they can connect prior to move-in day.

**Ken:** So it will be a male?

**Lucy:** Yes — an email.

**Ken:** No. A male. As in a man.

**Lucy:** Oh. Our halls are co-ed, but your son will share a dorm room with another guy. So yes?

**Ken:** Like a real male?

**Lucy:** What?

*(Beat.)*

**Ken:** Look, in my house, it’s simple. There are men and there are women. Girls and boys. Do you understand what I’m saying?

*(Lucy forces a small laugh.)*

**Lucy:** Sure. Can I help you with anything else, sir?

---

## Scene 4 – Escalation

**Ken:** How can I control what type of roommate my son gets?

**Lucy:** Control it?

**Ken (intensifying):** What process exactly does he go through?

**Lucy:** We send out a form to understand living habits, then pair people based on that.

**Ken:** Tell me more.

**Lucy (rambling):** I’d recommend answering honestly. Freshman year I thought I’d wake up at 8 a.m. every day, but—

**Ken:** I don’t really care. I’m here to talk about my son.

*(Beat.)*

**Ken:** I noticed you offer LGBTQ and all that stuff.

**Lucy:** I think you mean LGBTQ and our Gender Studies degree. What about it?

**Ken:** How do I restrict the classes he’s taking?

**Lucy:** Restrict?

**Ken:** Are there parental controls?

**Lucy:** His academic records are confidential. He can pick any classes he wants.

**Ken:** I’m paying for it! How can I see what he picks?

**Lucy:** He would have to give you his login.

**Ken:** Give it to me now—

**Lucy:** Sir, we can’t—

**Ken:** Hold on. Let me grab a pen and paper.

*(Door opens. Sam returns.)*

**Sam:** How’s it going?

**Lucy (whispering):** Help. Help. Help.

**Sam:** Relax. What’s going on?

**Lucy:** Homophobic control freak. Take it.

*(Rustling as she tries to pass the phone.)*

**Sara (off):** Lucy!

*(Lucy grabs the phone back.)*

---

## Scene 5 – Breaking Point

**Lucy (strained):** Hello?

**Ken:** I’m ready for that login.

**Lucy:** I can’t give you your son’s login. It’s private.

**Ken:** My son, exactly.

**Lucy:** That’s his private information.

**Ken:** Why did you make me get this pen and paper?!

**Lucy:** I didn’t—

**Ken:** What is your name?

**Lucy:** Lucy.

**Ken:** Spell that.

**Lucy:** L-U-C-Y.

*(Pen scribbling aggressively.)*

**Ken:** Now back to the classes. Why do you offer that?

**Lucy:** We offer a diverse education and believe students should study what they’re passionate about.

**Ken:** I don’t want to pay for that class.

**Lucy:** I can’t control what he enrolls in.

**Ken:** Put me on the phone with an adult.

**Lucy:** I am an adult.

**Ken:** Your supervisor. Now.

*(Call placed on hold. Light hold music beeps.)*

---

## Scene 6 – The Plan

**Sam:** That all sounds like fun.

**Lucy:** He wants to talk to Sara.

**Sam:** You can’t transfer him.

**Lucy:** He’s trying to control his son’s classes and roommate.

**Sam:** Do an accent.

**Lucy:** What?

**Sam:** Pretend you’re Sara.

*(Lucy inhales. Call taken off hold.)*

---

## Scene 7 – Fake Sara

**Lucy (southern accent):** Hi! Thanks for your patience. This is Sara. How can I help ya today?

**Ken:** Spell that.

*(Lucy covers mic.)*

**Lucy (to Sam):** Oh god.

**Lucy (accent):** S-A-R-A.

**Ken:** Your student worker said I can’t access my son’s account. Is that true?

**Lucy (accent):** That is correct.

**Ken:** What can you do about that?

**Lucy (accent):** Nothing. Lucy gave you the correct information.

**Ken:** That’s ridiculous!

**Lucy (accent):** It’s the law.

**Ken:** He’s my kid!

**Lucy (losing accent):** It’s the law, lady—

*(Beat.)*

**Lucy (back to accent):** Sir.

**Ken:** Is this a joke?

**Lucy (accent):** Will that be all for today?

**Ken:** Put your boss on the phone!

**Lucy:** Have a good day.

*(Click. Call hangs up.)*

---

## Scene 8 – Aftermath

**Sam:** See? You did great.

**Lucy:** I hung up on him. I impersonated our boss.

**Sam:** Maybe not your best work.

**Lucy:** I’m never taking a call again.

**Sam:** At least we’re in it together.

**Lucy:** Until Sara fires us. What did she say to you?

**Sam:** Where do I even begin?

*(Fade out.)*
